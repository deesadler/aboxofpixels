About Me

I am an UX/IxD user advocate.

I specialize in mobile UX and visual design.

Once upon a time I was a print designer, like many who started in this business. By 1998 I was doing web design and shortly after that I started training for a local Mac Specialist. That started both by web and training career.

By the early 2000's when I wasn't training I was doing web and UX, although I didn't have a name to it at that point. I would do some contracts every now for my favorite recruiter, and by 2007 I was working for Sprint as a UX Designer. Since then, UX has been by life. I even run an IxDA user group, because I believe in educating those who want to learn more about UX. I'm even speaking at Adobe MAX this year, doing a lecture/hands-on preconference day session on UX.

I believe in the UX process, because only then can you break the rules. I am a UX hybrid. I enjoy the strategy and talking with stakeholders. Standards matter to me and designing the familiar for the user. Wireframes, any old way they are needed. I also enjoy the visual design aspect and since I've done development in the past, working with the developers is lots of fun and making sure they have the spec docs or assets they need. I strongly believe the tools are just that, tools. I'm an expert in Adobe apps, but I am just as comfortable in Axure or whatever. Just as long as it truely is the right tool for the job. Workflow is key.

Workflow

Between people and apps

Bringing the right contributer at the right time in a project is essential and having a good design plan.

Same thing goes for the tools you use. If I can sketch, to wireframe, to clickable PDF, to prototype or visual design, all the better. I am more then familiar with the Adobe apps, Axure, Sketch and of course, post it's, index cards and pencil and paper.

Prototyping

User Testing

The correct prototype, is whatever interaction is needed for the user to correctly interact with the mobile/web/application being tested.

The tool used to create that prototype will also change depending on that interaction. I've used Muse, Flash, Fireworks, Indesign, HTML/js, InVisnionApp, UXPin and any number of other programs.

Mobile UX

IOS, Android, hybrid apps design/develop

I love designing mobile apps because "designing the familiar" is still being defined. Making something simple is harder then you think, so the challenge is exciting.

There are so many more varibles then desktop design. What is the person doing while using the app and what do they need to do are my favorite questions.
